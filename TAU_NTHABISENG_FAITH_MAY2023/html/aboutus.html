About Us

HISTORY

The Warner Bros website has a rich history that dates back to the early days of the internet. The website was first launched in 1995, and it quickly became a popular destination for movie and TV fans. At the time, the site featured basic information about Warner Bros' films and TV shows, as well as trailers and behind-the-scenes footage. Over the years, the Warner Bros website has evolved to keep up with changing technology and consumer expectations. In the early 2000s, the site added online stores where fans could purchase DVDs, Blu-rays, and other merchandise. In the late 2000s and early 2010s, the site became more interactive, with features like blogs, forums, and social media integration. Today, the Warner Bros website is a hub for all things related to Warner Bros' movies, TV shows, and video games. Fans can watch trailers and teasers, read news and reviews, and purchase tickets and merchandise. The site also features interactive experiences like virtual reality tours and augmented reality games. With its long and storied history, the Warner Bros website continues to be a go-to destination for entertainment fans around the world.

COMPANY MISSION

At Warner Bros., we are committed to creating and distributing world-class entertainment that engages, inspires and entertains audiences around the globe. Our mission is to provide our visitors with a dynamic and engaging online experience, showcasing our vast portfolio of film, television, and digital content, while fostering a community that celebrates creativity, diversity, and innovation. We strive to be a trusted and responsible corporate citizen, promoting positive social and environmental impact, and supporting the communities in which we operate. Our ultimate goal is to deliver the highest quality entertainment that enriches people's lives, inspires their imagination, and creates lasting memories

PRODUCT OR SERVICE DESCRIPTION

Movies: The website features information about the latest movies produced and distributed by Warner Bros. Users can find movie trailers, synopsis, and showtimes for movies that are currently playing in theaters.

TV Shows: Users can also find information about the latest TV shows produced by Warner Bros. The website offers episode guides, sneak peeks, and behind-the-scenes content.

Home Entertainment: Warner Bros also offers home entertainment products such as DVDs, Blu-rays, and digital downloads of their movies and TV shows. Users can purchase or rent these products directly from the website.

Games: Warner Bros produces and distributes video games for various platforms, including consoles, PC, and mobile devices. Users can find information about the latest games, trailers, and purchase them directly from the website.

Merchandise: Warner Bros offers a wide range of merchandise based on their popular movies and TV shows. Users can purchase products such as t-shirts, toys, and collectibles directly from the website.

Studio Tours: The website provides information about the Warner Bros studio tours, which allow visitors to explore the behind-the-scenes of their favorite movies and TV shows.

Corporate Information: The website also provides information about the company's corporate operations, including investor relations, press releases, and job openings.

AWARDS AND RECOGNITION

Academy Awards: Warner Bros. has won a total of 96 Academy Awards, including Best Picture wins for movies such as "Casablanca," "My Fair Lady," and "Million Dollar Baby."

Emmy Awards: The company has won a total of 365 Emmy Awards, including awards for its television shows such as "Friends," "The West Wing," and "Game of Thrones."

Golden Globe Awards: Warner Bros. has won a total of 139 Golden Globe Awards, including Best Picture wins for movies such as "A Star is Born," "The Departed," and "Argo."

Grammy Awards: The company has won a total of 16 Grammy Awards, including awards for its music recordings such as the "Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire" soundtrack and the "O Brother, Where Art Thou?" soundtrack.

BAFTA Awards: Warner Bros. has won a total of 72 BAFTA Awards, including Best Picture wins for movies such as "The King's Speech," "The Departed," and "Argo."

Screen Actors Guild Awards: The company has won a total of 42 Screen Actors Guild Awards, including awards for its performances in movies and television shows such as "The Sopranos," "ER," and "The Dark Knight."
